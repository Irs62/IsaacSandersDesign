Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

From this course, I have learned that every decision I make in design is communicating something, whether I realize it or not. The study of rhetoric has influenced my approach to my projects because I have been required to think about my audience, purpose, and emotional appeal of my design and writing. Technology has been a means of taking my message further, particularly when I was working in Canva, HTML, and other technologies. I have also become aware of the ethical issues in design and the responsibility I have as a designer to my audience, because my design will be viewed by many people with different backgrounds.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Some of the design principles I have learned and applied throughout this course include contrast, alignment, repetition, and proximity. These principles have helped me improve my design and create better layouts for my projects, whether I am making a magazine spread, a visual essay, or a portfolio page.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Peer workshops played a big part in my development. This is because not only was I able to share my work and receive feedback from others, but I was also able to provide constructive criticism to my peers. This helped me to view my work from a variety of viewpoints. In addition, it assisted me in learning how to express my opinions regarding my design in a clear and precise manner. Finally, it helped me to become more comfortable in presenting my opinions regarding my design decisions



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision became an integral part of my process. I revised texts, images, layouts, and web projects by reconsidering structure, typographic design, color schemes, and clarity. Switching between media, such as text, images, layouts, and web, allowed me to see how ideas evolve across media. I realized that revision is more than just error correction; it’s re-conceptualizing the work to suit the audience and purpose.

5. Learn to present your work.

This course has taught me how to present my work confidently and intentionally. I learned how to talk about my design choices, my design process, and how they relate to design principles. Creating my portfolio has helped me think about how to present my projects in a curated, cohesive way. I learned how to present my design to talk about my learning and process.

Accessibile Design

This project demonstrates my commitment to creating digital materials that are usable, intuitive, and welcoming for all learners. Grounded in the seven principles of Universal Design, my work focuses on removing barriers and ensuring that every user, regardless of ability, background, or experience—can access and understand the content. As shown in the document, Universal Design emphasizes that “the design is usable and appealing to all users” and that “experience or knowledge are not barriers to the design.” These ideas guided every decision I made.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Throughout this course, I was taught how design should not only be an artful process but an ethical one as well. With each assignment, I was challenged to consider my target audience, my intent, and the implications of my designs by thinking critically about how language, images, and technology influence perception and interaction.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

All semester long, I used basic design elements such as contrast, alignment, hierarchy, and proximity in order to create effective designs that were engaging and had visual unity. This allowed me to make informed decisions while ensuring I communicated through my designs effectively.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

It is essential to collaborate for this class. With peers’ reviews, critiques in groups, and workshops, I have learned to offer constructive feedback and improve myself. It was crucial for me to become a better designer and communicator.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision was an important process in all projects that I did. I gained a lot of experience in revising my documents through iteratively designing the layout of documents, enhancing language, and even redesigning my graphics for print and multimedia presentation.

5. Learn to present your work.

Through this class, I learned how to explain my design decisions and tell the story of each project. I did this by way of reflection writing, in-class presentations, or even in this portfolio.
